What is Open Handed Drumming?

July 31, 2024
blog
Open-handed drumming is a technique where a drummer plays with their dominant hand on the hi-hat and their non-dominant hand on the snare drum, rather than crossing their arms to play the hi-hat with their non-dominant hand. This allows for greater independence between the hands and can result in a more fluid and natural playing style. In addition to the technical benefits, open-handed drumming can also be beneficial for ergonomics and can reduce the risk of injury, as it allows the drummer to maintain a more natural posture and reduces the strain on the shoulders and back that can occur with crossed-arm playing.
