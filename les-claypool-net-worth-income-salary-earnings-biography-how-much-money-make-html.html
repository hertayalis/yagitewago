Primus bassist and lead singer Leslie Edward “Les” Claypool (born September 29, 1963) is a well-known American musician and writer, best known as their bassist and lead vocalist. Claypool’s electric bass playing style incorporates a variety of techniques like as tapping, flamenco-style strumming, whammy bar bending, and slapping. Claypool has also self-produced and engineered his solo albums, which he has done from his own studio, “Rancho Relaxo.” Claypool’s first full-length feature film, Electric Apricot, was released in 2006, as was his first book, South of the Pumphouse, which was written and directed by Claypool.
